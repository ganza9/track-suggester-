which coding track is best for you?

Front-end developers spend their time making things look and work well, obsessing over layouts, navigation, colors, and design. If this type of work appeals to you, your best bet is to take CSS and Design classes. If you're more interested in back-end development, you have several choices of languages: C#, Java, or Ruby. Most modern programming languages have more similarities than differences, and programmers often find themselves working in different languages than they originially studied. Back-end programmers often switch languages as they change jobs, or even as they build different types of software at the same job. So don't stress too much about making the right choice! That said, each language has found a bit of a niche, and depending on your interests, you might be attracted to one over another.
